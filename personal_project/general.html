The Generalist!

Generalists are bears that tend to make the most of whatever situation they find themself in. They are the bears that have that stereotypical life, they like honey, rummaging through garbage and of course commit the long sleep known as hibernation.

BLACK BEAR

Region: Western North America, Himalayas, Iran, Northern India, Korea, Eastern Russia, Japan, and Taiwan
Average Length: 160cm
Average Weight: 118kg
Viability: Technically speaking the black bear has two predominant archetypes, the north American black bear and the Asian black bear, however, their lifestyle and habitat are largely the same such I have categorized them as such. Black bears are the true master of adaptability, being omnivores that can eat virtually anything for energy. They have impeccable mobility and are the most agile of all the bears. They are notoriously great climbers and are very intelligent. Though humans continue to push into their terrain, black bears continue to adapt and have even turned human trash into treasure.
Rank: 10/10
The actual best bear, they can do virtually anything they need to and a just the perfect bear.

BROWN BEAR

Region: North America and Eurasia
Average Length: 210cm
Average Weight: 335kg
Viability: Brown bears are a physical powerhouse and are one of the strongest creatures in their environment, they are also the most intelligent bear, and thanks to their intelligence they can put all that strength to good use. The brown bear's main source of food is human trash and salmon. The brown bears' hunting of salmon is particularly ingenious as they use the salmon-required migration period to net a great deal of fat storage to make the bears' hibernation stunningly easy.
Rank: 7/10 - 10/10
Brown bears have many different styles, namely the grizzly or Kodiak, and depending on the style they are either some of the best bears out there or they have just a few things they lack

ANDEAN BEAR

Region: South American Andes
Average Length: 140cm
Average Weight: 100kg
Viability: Andean bears are the largest predator in the Andes giving them a unique place in the food chain. Though not quite apex predators, nothing in their region can hunt them, making their life relatively safe. Andean bears also have the largest size variations among the bears being able to be as small as 30kg and as large as 150kg. They are arboreal specialists with the fast climb speed of all the bears and insane vertical mobility. The only limiting factor to the Andean bear is the region they find themselves in, relying on insects and birds to provide them with fat for hibernation. This often results in not getting enough energy and instead must hunt throughout the winter.
Rank: 8/10
Andean Bears is held back more by its environment than anything else but is all-around a fantastic bear
